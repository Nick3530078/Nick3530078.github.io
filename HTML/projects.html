Projects

Don't Stop Speeding

Don't Stop Speeding is a video game that I and five other people created over the course of approximately four months. It was created in Unity and scripted with C#. The premise of the game is for players to earn as many points as possible before defusing the bomb in their car. As each game goes on, the player is able to purchase and utilize various upgrades in a randomly cycling shop in order to stay at the required speed that progressively gets higher over time. Moreover, the random generation offers gameplay variety and progressively gets more difficult over time.

Some of the gameplay mechanics that I created implementation for are as follows:

  • Random Generation
  • Upgrades
  • Store and Store Updating
  • Saving
  • Bomb Defusal
  • Score

Get Stuff Done

Get stuff Done is a website that I and approximately six other people created over the course of about three months. The website is composed of Python, HTML, CSS, and other technologies in an effort to help people, specifically students, manage their time more efficiently. The service allows users to create, edit, and delete tasks in a calendar. Each task is associated with a specific time as well as a category.

For the development of this project, I held the position of group president. As such, I not only helped to manage and organize our repository (GitHub), backlog, kanban, and such, but also ensured that each member of the team was supported in their work. Finally, I also aided in the development of the website itself such as writing code for automation and testing.
